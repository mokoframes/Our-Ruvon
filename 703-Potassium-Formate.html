Product Overview

Potassium Formate is an organic salt of formic acid. In the oilfield, it is valued for its ability to create heavy brines (up to 13.1 PPG or 1.57 SG) without the use of suspended solids like Barite. This prevents "formation damage" (clogging of oil-bearing rocks).

  • Low Corrosivity: Unlike Calcium Chloride or Bromide, Potassium Formate is much less corrosive to steel pipes and drilling equipment.
  • Thermal Stability: Remains stable at high temperatures (up to 218°C), making it ideal for deep, hot wells.
  • Environmentally Safe: It has low toxicity and low biological oxygen demand (BOD), making it safer for marine life in case of offshore spills.

The Eco-Friendly Heavy Weight

Why choose Formate over Chloride?

Potassium Formate provides two critical advantages in drilling:

  • Shale Stabilization: The Potassium ion ($K^+$) fits perfectly into the crystal lattice of clay/shale minerals. This locks the clay structure, preventing it from swelling and collapsing into the wellbore when it contacts water.
  • Green Chemistry: It does not contain chlorides (which rust equipment and harm soil). It breaks down naturally, posing minimal risk to the environment, allowing for easier disposal of drill cuttings.

Industrial Applications

Drilling & Completion Fluids

Used to create high-density, solids-free brine for well control and formation protection in HPHT (High Pressure High Temperature) wells.

Airport De-icing

Used as a non-corrosive, biodegradable de-icing agent for airport runways and taxiways (replaces urea and glycol).

Heat Transfer Fluid

Efficient secondary coolant for industrial refrigeration due to low viscosity at low temperatures.

Foliar Fertilizer

Source of Potassium for liquid fertilizers with high solubility.

Chemical Catalyst

Used as a catalyst or reagent in various organic synthesis reactions.

Safety & Handling Protocols

Safe & Biodegradable

Potassium Formate is considered non-toxic and environmentally friendly.

  • Hygroscopic: The powder form absorbs moisture rapidly. Keep bags tightly sealed.
  • Handling: Wear standard PPE (gloves, glasses). Wash with water if contact occurs.
  • Spill: Liquid spills can be slippery. Flush with plenty of water.
