Direct Blue 86

Direct Turquoise Blue GL (Liquid & Powder)

Direct Blue 86 is a premium direct dye known for its incredibly bright, brilliant turquoise shade. Unlike standard azo dyes, it is based on the Copper Phthalocyanine chromophore, which gives it exceptional stability against light and heat. It is the gold standard for dyeing cotton, viscose, and paper where brightness is key.

Product Overview

Direct Blue 86 is a substantive dye, meaning it has a natural affinity for cellulose fibers like cotton and paper pulp without requiring a mordant. However, adding salt (Sodium Sulfate/Chloride) helps exhaust the dye onto the fiber.

  • Brilliance: Produces a clean, greenish-blue (Cyan) shade that cannot be matched by other chemical classes.
  • Solubility: Excellent water solubility (up to 80 g/l), making it ideal for continuous dyeing and paper pulp coloring.
  • Economy: A cost-effective way to achieve bright turquoise shades on cellulosic fibers compared to Reactive dyes.

Chemistry: The "Copper Ring" Stability

Why is it so bright & stable?

Direct Blue 86 is chemically a Copper Phthalocyanine derivative. This molecule has a central Copper atom surrounded by a stable nitrogen ring system.

  • Light Fastness: This ring structure is incredibly resistant to UV degradation, giving the dye superior light fastness (6-7) compared to typical azo dyes (3-4).
  • Adsorption: The large, planar molecule aligns flat against the cellulose chain, held by strong Van der Waals forces and Hydrogen bonding, creating a brilliant color.

Industrial Applications

Paper Industry

Major Use: Used for dyeing tissue paper, napkins, and fine paper. It has excellent affinity for paper pulp and does not bleed easily.

Cotton & Viscose

Used for dyeing cotton fabrics, yarns, and curtains where bright turquoise shades are required at a low cost.

Inks & Stationery

Used in the formulation of water-based writing inks, highlighters, and inkjet inks due to high solubility.

Soaps & Detergents

Stable coloring agent for soap bars and detergent powders.

Agro-Chemicals

Used as a color marker for fertilizers and seed coatings.

Biological Stain

Used in microscopy as a contrast stain for certain tissues.

Safety & Handling Protocols

Dust Control

Direct dyes are fine powders that can generate dust.

  • Inhalation: Avoid breathing dust. Use local exhaust ventilation and wear a dust mask.
  • Staining: Intense colorant. Will stain skin and clothes immediately. Wear gloves and apron.
  • Storage: Hygroscopic. Store in a tightly sealed container in a cool, dry place.
