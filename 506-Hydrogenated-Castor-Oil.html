Product Overview

Hydrogenated Castor Oil (HCO), also known as Castor Wax, is chemically produced by adding hydrogen to pure castor oil in the presence of a nickel catalyst. This process saturates the double bonds, converting the liquid oil into a hard, white, waxy solid.

  • Stability: It retains the hydroxyl group (-OH) from castor oil, giving it unique chemical stability and compatibility with polar resins.
  • Hardness: It is exceptionally hard and brittle, with a much higher melting point than standard vegetable waxes.
  • Water Resistance: It imparts excellent water repellency to coatings and lubricants.

Science of Function: Thixotropy & Anti-Sagging

Why use HCO in Paints & Grease?

HCO acts as a powerful Thixotropic Agent. This means it creates a structure that is solid/gel-like when at rest, but becomes liquid when agitated (stirred/brushed).

  • Anti-Sagging: In paints, it prevents the coating from dripping or sagging down vertical walls after application.
  • Anti-Settling: It prevents heavy pigments from settling to the bottom of the paint can during storage.
  • Grease Structure: In Lithium Greases, it acts as the thickener that holds the lubricating oil in place, even at high temperatures.

Industrial Applications

Lubricating Greases

Crucial ingredient in Multi-Purpose Lithium Greases for automotive and industrial use.

Cosmetics

Used in stick products (lipsticks, deodorants) to provide structure and hardness.

Paints & Coatings

Rheology modifier for solvent-based paints, sealants, and caulks.

Polishes

Provides shine and water resistance in shoe creams and floor polishes.

Plastics & PVC

Internal lubricant for PVC processing to improve flow and surface finish.

Paper Coating

Used to impart gloss and water resistance to paper products.

Safety & Handling Protocols

Non-Hazardous Wax

HCO is generally safe and non-toxic.

  • Dust Hazard: Powder form can generate dust. Use dust masks and adequate ventilation.
  • Storage: Store in a cool, dry area away from direct sunlight. High temperatures can cause flakes to stick together (blocking).
  • Environment: Biodegradable and environmentally friendly.
