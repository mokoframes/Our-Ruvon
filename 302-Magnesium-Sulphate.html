Product Overview

Our Magnesium Sulphate is fully water-soluble, making it immediately available to plants. It has a neutral pH and does not alter soil acidity. It is particularly effective for crops that are heavy magnesium feeders like potatoes, tomatoes, roses, and peppers.

  • Dual Nutrient: Supplies both Magnesium (central to photosynthesis) and Sulfur (essential for protein synthesis).
  • High Solubility: Dissolves instantly in water, perfect for fertigation and hydroponics.
  • Corrects Chlorosis: Rapidly fixes yellowing between leaf veins (interveinal chlorosis) caused by Mg deficiency.

The Science: "Green Blood" of Plants

[Image of chlorophyll molecule structure]
Why Magnesium is the Heart?

Just as Iron (Fe) is the central atom in human hemoglobin (Red Blood), Magnesium (Mg) is the central atom in the Chlorophyll molecule (Green Blood).

  • Photosynthesis: Without Magnesium, plants cannot produce chlorophyll, meaning they cannot convert sunlight into energy. This leads to weak growth and poor yield.
  • Enzyme Activator: Magnesium activates key enzymes needed for plant growth and aids in the uptake of Phosphorus ($P$), boosting root development.

Applications

Foliar Spray

Sprayed directly on leaves to quickly cure yellowing. The most efficient way to deliver Magnesium.

Soil Application

Mixed with soil before planting to ensure long-term availability of Mg and S nutrients.

Hydroponics

A critical component of nutrient solutions in soilless farming due to its high purity and solubility.

Pharma & Personal Care

Used as "Bath Salts" to relax muscles (absorbs through skin) and as a saline laxative.

Textile Industry

Used in weighting cotton and silk, and as a fixative in dyeing processes.

Pulp & Paper

Used in the Kraft process to improve pulp brightness and strength.

Storage: Magnesium Sulphate absorbs moisture from the air (Hygroscopic). Store in a cool, dry place. If it cakes, it can be easily broken up and dissolved without loss of efficacy.

Safety & Handling Protocols

Non-Toxic & Safe

Magnesium Sulphate is generally safe but should be handled with care.

  • Ingestion: Acts as a laxative if swallowed in large amounts. Keep away from children.
  • Dust: Can irritate eyes and respiratory tract. Wear a mask when handling bulk powder.
  • Eco-Friendly: It is a natural mineral and safe for the environment when used as directed.
