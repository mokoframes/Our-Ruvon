Product Overview

SLES 70% is preferred over SLS (Sodium Lauryl Sulfate) because it is much milder on the skin and eyes, thanks to the ethoxylation process. It has superior resistance to hard water and high biodegradability.

  • Thickening Response: SLES responds incredibly well to Sodium Chloride (Common Salt), allowing manufacturers to easily adjust the viscosity (thickness) of shampoos and liquid soaps.
  • Rich Lather: Produces dense, creamy foam that consumers associate with high-quality cleaning.
  • Solubility: Easily soluble in water, forming a clear transparent liquid.

Mechanism: The "Micelle Magic"

How does it actually clean?

SLES molecules are like tiny magnets with two ends:

  • The Head (Hydrophilic): Loves water. It pulls the molecule into the rinse water.
  • The Tail (Lipophilic): Loves oil/dirt. It hates water.
  • The Micelle: When you wash, millions of tails stick into the dirt/oil, while heads face the water. They form a ball called a Micelle, trapping the dirt inside. When you rinse with water, the heads pull the whole ball (dirt included) away from your skin/plate.

Pro-Tip: Avoiding the "Gel Block"

Handling SLES 70% Paste

SLES 70% is unique. If you add Water into Paste, it will form a hard, rubbery gel that is impossible to dissolve. This is called the "Gelling Phenomenon."
The Solution: Always add Paste into Water while stirring continuously. This ensures smooth dilution without lumps. Also, use warm water (30-40°C) for faster dissolution.
