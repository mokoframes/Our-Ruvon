Product Overview

Ruvon's Activated Carbon is produced by steam activation of premium coconut shells. This process creates a carbon with high hardness and low dust, which is critical for gold recovery and water filters where breakdown of granules leads to pressure drop and loss of material.

  • Adsorption Capacity: Measured by Iodine Value (IV) for liquids and CTC for gases. We offer grades from 600 IV to 1300 IV.
  • Purity: Low Ash content ensures minimal impurities leaching into the treated water or process stream.
  • Durability: Coconut shell carbon is significantly harder and more abrasion-resistant than coal or wood-based carbons.

The Micro-Pore Advantage

Surface Area of a Football Field?

Yes. Just 5 grams of Activated Carbon has the surface area equivalent to a football field! This is due to the Microporous Structure.

  • The Trap: Coconut shell carbon creates pores < 2nm in size. These are perfectly sized to trap small molecules like Chlorine, Pesticides, and Solvents via Van der Waals forces.
  • Gold Recovery: In mining, gold-cyanide complexes are adsorbed into these pores. The superior hardness of coconut shell prevents the carbon from grinding into dust during the agitation process, saving millions in lost gold.

Industrial Applications

Water Treatment

Removal of chlorine, taste, odor, and organic contaminants (TOC) from drinking and waste water.

Gold Recovery

Used in CIP (Carbon-in-Pulp) and CIL (Carbon-in-Leach) processes to extract gold from ore.

Air & Gas Purification

Removal of toxic gases, VOCs, and odors in respirators, HVAC systems, and industrial scrubbers.

Food & Beverage

Decolorization of sugar syrup, purification of edible oils, and removal of impurities in alcohol/vodka.

Pharmaceuticals

Purification of APIs and treatment of poisoning (medicinal charcoal).

Safety & Handling Protocols

Low Oxygen Hazard

Wet activated carbon depletes oxygen from the air.

  • Confined Spaces: Do not enter enclosed tanks containing wet carbon without oxygen monitoring equipment. It can cause suffocation.
  • Dust: Carbon dust is nuisance dust. Wear a mask to prevent inhalation and black sputum.
  • Combustible: Although hard to ignite, carbon dust can be explosive in high concentrations. Keep away from open flames.
