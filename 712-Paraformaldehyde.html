Paraformaldehyde

96% Powder (H2S Scavenger & Biocide)

Paraformaldehyde (Paraform) is the polymerized solid form of Formaldehyde with a high active content of 96%+. In the Oil & Gas sector, it is the most efficient and economical Hydrogen Sulfide ($H_2S$) Scavenger. It also acts as a potent biocide to control bacterial growth in drilling muds, preventing reservoir souring.

Product Overview

Ruvon supplies premium Paraformaldehyde which depolymerizes upon heating or in alkaline conditions to release Formaldehyde gas. This gas reacts instantly with sulfides. It serves as a cost-effective alternative to liquid formalin (37%) because it contains negligible water, reducing transport and storage costs by 60%.

  • H2S Removal: Effectively removes Hydrogen Sulfide from drilling fluids, preventing corrosion and ensuring personnel safety.
  • Biocidal Activity: Kills bacteria, fungi, and algae in cooling towers and oil wells.
  • Resin Manufacturing: Key raw material for Phenolic, Melamine, and Urea resins used in plywood and coatings.

Mechanism: The Neutralization Reaction

How does it eat H2S?

When Paraformaldehyde is added to a drilling mud containing lethal $H_2S$ gas, it breaks down into monomeric Formaldehyde.

  • The Reaction: 3 HCHO + 3 $H_2S$ $\rightarrow$ Trithiane + 3 $H_2O$.
  • The Result: The deadly gas is converted into Trithiane, a stable, non-toxic solid precipitate that is easily removed from the mud. This reaction is irreversible, meaning the $H_2S$ will not return.

Pro-Tip: Prills vs Powder

Which form to choose?

Powder: Dissolves faster due to high surface area. Best for immediate reaction in resin manufacturing or emergency H2S spikes.
Prills (Beads): Free-flowing and less dusty. Easier to handle and store. Preferred for steady dosing in drilling operations where "Dust Explosion" risk needs to be minimized.

Industrial Applications

Oilfield Scavenger

Removes sulfides from drilling muds, protecting workers and preventing pipe corrosion.

Resin Production

Reactant for Phenol-Formaldehyde, Urea-Formaldehyde, and Melamine resins (Laminates/Adhesives).

Disinfectant / Biocide

Used to fumigate poultry farms, hatcheries, and sterilize medical equipment.

Paints & Coatings

Used in alkyd resins and as a hardening agent for casein and gelatin.

Chemical Synthesis

Raw material for Glyphosate (Herbicide), Vitamin A, and other fine chemicals.

Sugar Industry

Used as a bactericide to prevent sugar loss during juice processing.

Safety & Handling Protocols

Flammable & Toxic

Paraformaldehyde releases Formaldehyde, a known carcinogen.

  • Inhalation: Causes severe irritation to respiratory tract. Always use a full-face respirator or work in a fume hood.
  • Dust: Paraform dust is combustible. Avoid sparks and open flames near storage areas.
  • Eyes: Causes serious eye damage. Wear chemical safety goggles.
