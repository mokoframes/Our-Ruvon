Product Overview

Unlike liquid bleach (Sodium Hypochlorite) which degrades quickly, Stable Bleaching Powder (SBP) retains its chlorine content for a long period if stored correctly. It releases nascent oxygen and chlorine when dissolved in water, which acts as a powerful oxidizer to kill bacteria, viruses, and algae.

  • High Potency: 1 kg of SBP contains as much chlorine as 3-4 liters of standard liquid bleach.
  • Cost Effective: The most economical method for mass chlorination of water supplies.
  • Multi-Purpose: Used for bleaching textiles, sanitizing floors, and treating aquaculture ponds.

Mechanism: The Chemistry of HOCl

How does it Kill Germs?

When Calcium Hypochlorite dissolves in water, it hydrolyzes to form Hypochlorous Acid (HOCl).

  • The Penetrator: HOCl is electrically neutral (no charge). Because bacterial cell walls are negatively charged, they repel charged ions (like OCl-). However, neutral HOCl penetrates the cell wall easily.
  • Oxidation: Once inside, it oxidizes the bacteria's enzymes and DNA, killing it instantly. This is why "Available Chlorine" matters—it determines how much HOCl is generated.

Safety & Handling Protocols

DANGER: Oxidizer & Corrosive

Chlorine gas is toxic. Handle with extreme care.

  • Incompatibility: NEVER mix with acids (like toilet cleaner) or ammonia. This releases deadly chlorine gas immediately.
  • Storage: Store in a cool, dry, well-ventilated place away from heat, oil, and grease (fire hazard).
  • PPE: Wear gas mask/respirator, rubber gloves, and eye protection.
