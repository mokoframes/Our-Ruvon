Product Overview

Glycerine is a "Tri-hydric Alcohol" derived naturally. It is odorless but has a sweet taste (60% as sweet as sugar). Its key property is Hygroscopy, meaning it absorbs moisture from the air, making it indispensable for keeping products moist and soft.

  • Safe & Pure: Our USP grade is free from diethylene glycol (DEG) and ethylene glycol, ensuring safety for cough syrups and oral intake.
  • Solvent: Excellent solvent for food flavorings and colors where alcohol cannot be used.
  • Emollient: Softens and smoothes skin in lotions and soaps.

Mechanism: The Hygroscopic Magnet

Why is it "Tri-Hydric"?

Glycerine has Three Hydroxyl (-OH) Groups in its chemical structure. Propylene Glycol has only two.

  • Triple Moisture Lock: These three groups act like super-magnets for water molecules. They form strong hydrogen bonds with water from the air.
  • Result: When applied to skin or added to a cake, Glycerine holds onto water tightly, preventing the product from drying out even in dry conditions. This makes it a superior humectant to PG.

Industrial Applications

Pharmaceuticals

Base for cough syrups, elixirs, and suppositories. Acts as a sweetener and solvent.

Cosmetics & Soaps

Primary ingredient in moisturizing lotions, transparent soaps, and toothpaste.

Food & Beverage

E422 additive. Keeps cakes moist, acts as a solvent for food colors, and sweetener for diabetic foods.

Tobacco & Vaping

Keeps tobacco moist and is the base liquid (VG) for e-cigarettes.

Alkyd Resins

Reacted with dibasic acids to produce alkyd resins for paints and coatings.

Antifreeze

Used as a non-toxic alternative to ethylene glycol in antifreeze applications.

Storage: Glycerine is extremely hygroscopic. If the lid is left open, it will absorb water from the air, lowering its purity percentage. Keep tightly sealed.

Safety & Handling Protocols

Non-Toxic & GRAS

Vegetable Glycerine is Generally Recognized As Safe (GRAS).

  • Edible: It is safe for consumption in regulated amounts.
  • Flammability: Combustible at high temperatures (Flash Point > 160°C).
  • Spills: Spilled glycerine is extremely slippery. Wash down with plenty of water.
