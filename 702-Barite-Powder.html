Product Overview

Our Barite is chemically inert, non-abrasive, and has a high specific gravity. It does not react with other drilling fluid additives and does not interfere with magnetic measurements in the borehole.

  • Density Control: Can increase mud weight up to 20 lb/gal (2.40 SG) to counterbalance high formation pressures.
  • Inertness: Insoluble in water and oil, ensuring it remains stable in both Water-Based Muds (WBM) and Oil-Based Muds (OBM).
  • Low Abrasiveness: Soft enough (Mohs hardness ~3) to minimize wear and tear on drilling bits and pumps.

Mechanism: The Hydrostatic Shield

How does it stop a blowout?

Drilling allows pressurized gas/oil to try and escape up the hole. To stop this, we fill the hole with "Mud."

  • Hydrostatic Pressure: The weight of the mud column creates downward pressure. Formula: P = 0.052 x Mud Weight x Depth.
  • Barite's Role: Water weighs 8.3 ppg. To fight deep pressures, we need 15+ ppg. Adding Barite increases this weight significantly without making the mud too thick to pump. It acts as a heavy lid on a pressure cooker.

Pro-Tip: Rheology & Barite Sag

Preventing "Sag"

Barite Sag occurs when heavy Barite particles settle down in the well when pumping stops. This can cause stuck pipes or pressure loss.
Solution: Ensure your Barite has the correct particle size distribution (PSD). Too many coarse particles (>75 microns) cause settling. Too many fines (<6 microns) increase viscosity too much (Plastic Viscosity). Our API grade balances this perfectly.

Industrial Applications

Oil & Gas Drilling

90% of global use. Weighting agent for drilling fluids in exploration wells.

Friction Materials

Used as an inert filler in brake linings and clutch pads to increase density and heat resistance.

Paints & Coatings

High-quality filler (Blanc Fixe) for primers and industrial paints. Provides chemical resistance.

Radiation Shielding

Used in high-density concrete for hospitals and nuclear plants to block X-rays and Gamma rays.

Plastics & Rubber

Filler to increase weight and sound-deadening properties in plastics and acoustic foams.

Glass Industry

Used as a flux to lower the melting point and improve clarity in glass manufacturing.

Safety & Handling Protocols

Non-Toxic Dust

Barium Sulfate is generally insoluble and non-toxic, unlike soluble Barium salts.

  • Inhalation: Prolonged inhalation of dust can cause Baritosis (benign pneumoconiosis). Use dust masks.
  • Environment: Our material is tested for heavy metals (Cd, Hg) to ensure it is safe for marine discharge (Offshore).
