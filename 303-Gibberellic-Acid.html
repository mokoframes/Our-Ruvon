Product Overview

Gibberellic Acid (GA3) is a pentacyclic diterpene acid. It is produced industrially by the fermentation of the fungus Gibberella fujikuroi. Ruvon International supplies high-purity Technical Grade GA3 (90% Min), which serves as the active ingredient for various agricultural formulations (SP, EC, Tablets).

Unlike fertilizers that provide nutrients, GA3 acts as a hormonal signal to the plant, triggering rapid growth responses such as stem elongation, uniform flowering, and fruit setting even under adverse conditions. It is particularly famous for producing seedless grapes and increasing sugarcane yield.

The "Growth Hormone"

Gibberellic Acid works by stimulating cell division and elongation. In Grapes, it elongates the bunch and increases berry size, creating high-value table grapes. In Sugarcane, it increases the internode length, significantly boosting biomass and sugar yield per acre. For Seed Production, it breaks dormancy in seeds and tubers, ensuring uniform and rapid germination, which is critical for commercial nurseries.

Agricultural Uses

Viticulture

Crucial for producing seedless grapes and increasing berry size.

Sugarcane

Increases stalk length and overall tonnage yield.

Seed Treatment

Breaks dormancy in potatoes and other seeds for fast sprouting.

Fruit Orchards

Delays fruit aging (senescence) in citrus, extending shelf life.

Malting

Accelerates the malting process of barley in brewing industry.

Vegetables

Promotes bolting and flowering in biennial crops.

Safety & Handling Protocols

Plant Hormone Safety

GA3 is a naturally occurring compound and has low toxicity to humans and animals.

  • Storage: Store in a cool, dark, dry place. Direct sunlight can degrade the potency of the hormone.
  • PPE: Wear dust mask and gloves to prevent inhalation of fine powder or skin contact.
  • Dosage: Strictly follow recommended ppm (parts per million) dosage; overdose can cause excessive, weak growth.
