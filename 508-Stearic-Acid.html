Product Overview

Stearic Acid is a solid, white crystalline fatty acid. Commercially, it is a blend of Stearic Acid (C18) and Palmitic Acid (C16). The ratio of C18 to C16 determines the grade (Triple Pressed vs. Rubber Grade) and its melting point properties.

  • Triple Pressed: High purity, low iodine value, white color. Used in Pharma and Cosmetics.
  • Rubber Grade: Specifically designed to activate accelerator systems in rubber compounding.
  • Lubricity: Excellent internal and external lubricant for PVC and other plastics.

Functionality: The Vulcanization Activator

Role in Rubber & Tires

In the rubber industry, Stearic Acid is indispensable. It does not cure the rubber itself, but it activates the curing system.

  • Reaction: It reacts with Zinc Oxide to form Zinc Stearate in situ during the mixing process.
  • Activation: This soluble Zinc Stearate then reacts with the accelerator to speed up Vulcanization (Cross-linking).
  • Result: Without Stearic Acid, rubber products would be soft, sticky, and lack tensile strength.

Industrial Applications

Rubber & Tyres

Activator and dispersing agent for carbon black in tire manufacturing.

Personal Care

Pearlizing agent in shampoos and thickener/emulsifier in creams (Vanishing Cream).

Plastics (PVC)

Acts as an external lubricant and heat stabilizer in PVC pipe processing.

Candles

Increases melting point and hardness of wax, improving burn time and opacity.

Soaps & Detergents

Used to harden soaps and give a creamy lather.

Metallic Stearates

Raw material for producing Zinc Stearate, Calcium Stearate, and Magnesium Stearate.

Storage Note: Stearic acid is stable. Store in a cool, dry place away from direct heat sources to prevent caking or discoloration.

Safety & Handling Protocols

Handling Guidelines

Generally considered non-toxic, but dust can be an irritant.

  • Dust Explosion: Finely powdered stearic acid can form explosive dust clouds. Ensure good ventilation and grounding.
  • Melting: Use indirect heating (steam/oil) to melt. Direct flame can cause scorching and degradation.
  • PPE: Wear dust mask and gloves during handling.
