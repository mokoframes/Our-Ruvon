Product Overview

Most calcium sources (like Lime or Gypsum) are not soluble in water and take months to work. Calcium Nitrate dissolves instantly. It acts as a structural nutrient, strengthening the skin of fruits and vegetables, preventing rotting, and extending shelf life after harvest.

  • Immediate Uptake: Contains Nitrate Nitrogen ($NO_3$), which is the preferred nitrogen form for plants, unlike Urea which requires soil conversion.
  • Boron Boost: Added Boron improves pollination, seed set, and ensures Calcium reaches the fruit.
  • Salinity Management: Calcium displaces harmful Sodium ($Na$) from the soil, improving soil structure in salty lands.

The Science: Ca + B Synergy

Why combine Calcium and Boron?

Calcium is a "Lazy Nutrient." It enters the plant roots but struggles to move up to the fruits and flowers where it is needed most.

  • The Driver: Boron acts as the "transport manager." It facilitates the movement of sugars and Calcium to the growing tips and fruits.
  • Brick & Mortar: If Calcium is the "Brick" of the cell wall, Boron is the "Mortar/Cement." Without Boron, the cell walls remain weak, leading to fruit cracking. Together, they create a skin so strong that pests and fungus cannot penetrate easily.

The "Deficiency Detective"

Signs You Need Calcium Nitrate
  • Blossom End Rot: Tomatoes and peppers turning black/rotten at the bottom.
  • Fruit Cracking: Pomegranate, Grapes, and Tomatoes splitting open before harvest.
  • Bitter Pit: Small dark spots on Apples.
  • Tip Burn: Edges of lettuce or cabbage leaves turning brown and drying.
Benefits of Usage
  • Longer Shelf Life: Fruits stay fresh longer during transport and storage.
  • Crunchiness: Improves the texture and "crunch" of fruits like apples and cucumbers.
  • Disease Resistance: Stronger cell walls prevent fungal attacks.

Application Methods

Drip Fertigation

Dose: 3-5 kg per Acre.

Dissolve in the tank and apply during the fruit-setting stage. Essential for horticultural crops.

Foliar Spray

Dose: 4-5 grams per Liter.

Spray on leaves and fruits during early morning. Provides an instant calcium boost to prevent cracking.

Hydroponics

Primary Source:

The main source of Nitrogen and Calcium in Part A of hydroponic nutrient solutions. Must be kept separate from Sulphates/Phosphates.
